Information Retrieval in RDF-Data Survey

This survey is a part of the master thesis of Martin Gjerde and Ebba Fingarsen. It aims to compare 3 different search methods on two datasets of structured RDF-data.

We will not keep any information about who is completing the survey. The goal of the survey is not to test you, and there are no right or wrong answers. The goal is to assess the users' percieved performance of different search methods. Please read this page carefully before you start the survey.

What will you do?

You will be presented with a query and the information need/intent behind it, and the top 10 results for the given query. These results are entities with a small amount of information. Your task is to rate how relevant you perceive each result in accordance with the intent.

You will rate the results for each query a total of three times, one for each search method. This means that you might encounter the same results multiple times, but in a different order.

Each result should be ranked with a score on a scale of 0-3. A score of 3 corresponds to an exact match between the entity and the intent. A score of 2 corresponds to results which do not completely match the intent, but are still higly relevant. 1 corresponds to a result that does not match the intent, but still partially mathces query keywords. Finally, 0 corresponds to trivial, or nonsense results.

Example

Below you can see an example for four rated results. In this query we want to retrieve J.R.R Tolkien's Lord of the rings novels

Query: Tolkien lord of the rings novels

Intent: Retrieve novels in the lord of the rings series written by J.R.R Tolkien

The Fellowship of the Rings

Description:1954 book by J. R. R. Tolkien

Alternative names: Lord of the rings, lotr

The Hobbit

Description:fantasy novel by J. R. R. Tolkien

Alternative names: Hobbit, There and Back Again

Lord of the Flies

Description:1954 novel by William Golding

Charlie and the chocholat factory

Description:1964 book by Roald Dahl

The first result is given a score of 3 because it fulfills the query intent.
The second result is given a score of 2 because it is not a lord of the rings novel, but still a relevant book written by the same author.
The third result is given a score of 1 because it is not a relevant result, but the enitity name is similar to the query keywords.
The fourth result is given a score of 0 because it is not a relevant result and does not really match the query keywords.

How long will it take?

The survey consists of two different datasets containing five queries each. You will therefore go through five queries with three methods each. This is a total of 15 result sets to be rated for each dataset. Each result set is represented by a maximum of 10 results.

You can at any time take a break and come back to the survey at a later point if you wish to do so. All completed queries assists our evaluation. The estimated time for completion is 30min, but the time used may vary.
