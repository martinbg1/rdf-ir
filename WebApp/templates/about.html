Information Retrieval in RDF-Data Survey

This survey is a part of the master thesis of Martin Gjerde and Ebba Fingarsen. It aims to compare 3 different search methods on two datasets of structured RDF-data.

We will not keep any information about who is completing the survey. The goal of the survey is not to test you, and there are no right or wrong answers. The goal is to assess the users' percieved performance of different search methods.

What will you do?

You will be presented with a query and the information need/intent behind the query. Your task is to rate how relevant you perceive each result

Each result should be ranked with a score on a scale of 0-3 where 0 is not relevant and 3 is relevant. Each result has to be given a rating before you can submit the answer.

Below you can see an example for three rated results. In this query we want to retrieve J.R.R Tolkien Lord of the ring novels

Query: Tolkien lord of the ring novels

Intent: Retrieve novels in the lord of the rings series written by J.R.R Tolkien

Lord of the Flies

How long will it take?

The survey consists of X queries distributed equally on two datasets. Each query is represented by a maximum of 10 results.

You can at any time take a break and come back to the survey at a later point if you wish to do so. All completed queries assists our evaluation. The estimated time for completion is Xmin, but the time used may vary.
